Core Elements of Forest Kindergarten
Child-led Flow Learning
Child led learning is when a child chooses the activity rather than the adult. The child has control of the activity but the nursery practitioner can adapt their language to gain learning and further the child's development.
Child-inspired, child-directed documentation of Emergent Curriculum
Emergent curriculum is defined as a process where teachers plan activities and projects based on the specific group of children they are working with, taking into account their skills, needs, and interests.
Inquiry-Based Teaching Style
Inquiry-based learning is an approach to learning that emphasizes the student's role in the learning process. Rather than the teacher telling students what they need to know, students are encouraged to explore the material, ask questions, and share ideas.
Place-based Education
Place-Based Education is an approach that connects learning and communities with the primary goals of increasing student engagement, boosting academic outcomes, impacting communities and promoting understanding of the world around us.

Membership Levels
Individual

Membership applies to one person, and this is best suited for an interested party and/or advocate of forest kindergartens. Benefits received is very similar to the Educator level, content is slightly tailored to reflect this audience.

Educator

Membership applies to one person. They receive access to our members-only Facebook group, first peek at content developed by our expert board of directors, placement on the AFKA forest kindergartens map of programs and use of the AFKA logo on their personal/program website

Organization

Membership can include yourself and other members of your staff. Otherwise, the benefits received are the same as the Educator level.
